Population Dynamics Models
An in-depth exploration of predator-prey and logistic growth models through differential equations, combining mathematical analysis with computational simulations in Python. Discover how equilibrium points and phase portraits reveal the complexities of ecological systems and inform sustainable resource management.
Project Details / Background
This project was my research paper (m√©moire) for the end of the first year of Master's (M1). It involved a detailed mathematical study of classical population dynamics models, focusing on the Volterra-Lotka predator-prey system and the Verhulst logistic growth equation. I manually calculated the equilibrium points and analyzed their stability to deeply understand the system behavior.
To complement the theoretical work, I implemented numerical simulations and phase portrait visualizations using Python. These computational models were essential to validate my analytical results and supported the development of an original hybrid model combining predator-prey interactions with logistic growth, further explored through simulations.
The complete hybrid model I studied was described by the following system of differential equations: $$ \begin{cases} \frac{dx}{dt} = ax \left(1 - \frac{x}{M}\right) - bxy - qEx \\ \frac{dy}{dt} = -cy + dxy - \tilde{q}\tilde{E}y \end{cases} $$ where:
  • $a$ is the intrinsic growth rate of the prey.
  • $b$ is the predation rate coefficient.
  • $c$ is the natural death rate of the predators.
  • $d$ is the reproduction rate of predators based on prey consumed.
  • $M$ is the environmental carrying capacity for the prey.
  • $E, \tilde{E}$ represent fishing effort on prey and predator respectively.
  • $q, \tilde{q}$ are catchability coefficients for each species.
This project significantly enhanced my knowledge of differential equations and provided me with my first hands-on experience in population dynamics modeling. The work culminated in a comprehensive report of over 50 pages detailing the theoretical and practical insights gained.
